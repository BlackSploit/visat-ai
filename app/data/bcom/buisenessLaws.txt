SEMESTER 1: CORPORATE REGULATIONS AND COMPLIANCE
Course Code: MG1DSCBLW100 | Credits: 4 | Total Hours: 75

Course Summary
Equips students with the knowledge of Indian Corporate Law based on the Companies Act 2013. Covers corporate administration, incorporation, governance, and the role of the Company Secretary.

Course Outcomes
Understand key concepts related to companies.

Analyse procedures for incorporation.

Understand duties of directors, shareholders, and promoters.

Apply provisions to transactions, meetings, and winding up.

Understand the role of a Company Secretary.

Communicate legal issues professionally.

Course Content
Module 1: Company Legal Background & Incorporation

Characteristics and history of Company Law; NCLT, NCLAT, and SFIO.

Kinds of Companies: Private, Public, OPC, Section 8, LLP, Foreign, Government, Holding/Subsidiary.

Lifting the Corporate Veil; Promoters' roles.

Memorandum of Association (MoA) & Articles of Association (AoA): Contents, Alteration, Ultravires, Indoor Management.

Prospectus: Types, formalities, and misrepresentation.

Module 2: Shares, Membership, and Administration

Shares: Types, Transfer, Transmission, and Share Capital alteration.

Debentures: Fixed and Floating charges; Crystallisation.

Membership: Modes of acquisition, rights, and privileges.

Directors: Appointment, qualifications, duties, and liabilities.

Company Secretary: Role, position, and responsibilities.

Module 3: Administration, Winding Up, and Governance

Company Meetings: Kinds, requisites of a valid meeting, and resolutions.

Winding Up: Meaning, types, and role of Official Liquidator.

Corporate Governance & CSR: Importance and statutory provisions.

Module 4: Practical Formalities (CCA)

Preparation of MoA, AoA, and Prospectus.

Online registration via MCA site (DSC, DIN, MCA 21).

Drafting Notice, Agenda, and Minutes.

Virtual meetings, Polls, and Proxies.

SEMESTER 2: BUSINESS REGULATORY FRAMEWORK
Course Code: MG2DSCBLW100 | Credits: 4 | Total Hours: 75

Course Summary
Familiarizes students with commercial and mercantile laws governing business transactions, specifically the Indian Contract Act and Sale of Goods Act.

Course Content
Module 1: Introduction to Mercantile Law

Sources of Law.

Indian Contract Act: Essentials of a valid contract, Offer, Acceptance, Consideration, Capacity, and Free Consent.

Module 2: Legality and Performance of Contracts

Void contracts, Quasi-contracts, Contingent contracts, and E-contracts.

Performance, Discharge, and Breach of Contract (Remedies).

Module 3: Special Contracts

Bailment and Pledge: Rights and duties of parties.

Indemnity and Guarantee: Kinds of guarantee, rights/liabilities of surety.

Agency: Creation, types of agents, and termination.

Module 4: Sale of Goods Act

Contract of Sale vs. Agreement to Sell.

Conditions and Warranties; Caveat Emptor.

Transfer of ownership and performance.

Unpaid Seller: Rights and Auction sales.

SEMESTER 3 & 4: GST LAWS: FUNDAMENTALS
Course Code: MG3/4DSCBLW200 | Credits: 4 | Total Hours: 75

Course Summary
Overview of GST in the Indian Taxation system, covering principles, practical applications, and operational aspects.

Course Content
Module 1: Introduction to GST & Supply

Evolution and Constitutional background.

Structure of GST (CGST, SGST, IGST), GST Council, and GSTN.

Concepts: Aggregate turnover, RCM, ECO, HSN/SAC codes.

Scope and Types of Supply.

Module 2: Time, Place, and Value of Supply

Provisions and practical situations for determining Time, Place, and Value of supply.

Module 3: Input Tax Credit (ITC), Invoice, and E-Way Bills

Manner of claiming ITC and benefits.

Tax Invoice types; Composition Scheme, TDS, and TCS.

E-Way Bill provisions.

Module 4: Practical Aspects of GST

Familiarizing with the GST Common Portal.

Generating E-Way bills and GST-compliant invoices.

GST Registration process and Compliance Rating.

ASSESSMENT STRUCTURE
Continuous Comprehensive Assessment (CCA): 30 Marks

Presentations, Drafting reports, Mock meetings/trials, MCQ tests, and Field visits.

End Semester Examination: 70 Marks

Section A: 20 MCQs (1 mark each).

Section B: 25 MCQs (2 marks each).
