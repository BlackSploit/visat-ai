COURSE DETAILS
1. Principles and Practices of Management (MG1CCRBBA100)
Credits: 4 | Core Course

Summary: Introduces key aspects of management: planning, organizing, leading, and controlling, integrating classical and contemporary practices.

Course Outcomes:

Understand management principles to solve business problems.

Contrast management theories (Scientific, Administrative, Bureaucratic, etc.).

Design management strategies and propose innovative solutions.

Course Content:

Unit 1: Introduction: Definition, nature, and significance. Levels of management and managerial skills. Evolution of thought: Classical, Human Relations, Quantitative, Systems, and Contingency approaches.

Unit 2: Planning & Organizing: Types of plans (Strategic, Tactical, Operational). Decision-making steps. Organizational structures (Functional, Divisional, Matrix). Delegation, Centralization vs. Decentralization. MBO & MBE. Staffing (Recruitment and Selection).

Unit 3: Directing & Controlling: Leadership, Motivation, and Communication. Control process, essentials of sound control, and methods.

Unit 4: Strategy & Ethics: SWOT analysis, Ethical issues in management, and Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR).

2. Financial Accounting (MG1CCRBBA101)
Credits: 4 | Core Course

Summary: Introduces basic accounting principles, the accounting cycle (Journal to Final Accounts), and sustainability accounting.

Course Outcomes:

Identify principles for preparing financial statements.

Demonstrate the accounting cycle process.

Prepare Final Accounts for sole proprietorships and understand Company formats.

Course Content:

Unit 1: Introduction: Accounting system/info, Concepts and Conventions, Capital vs. Revenue, Accounting Equation.

Unit 2: Recording Transactions: Rules of Debit/Credit, Journal, Ledger, Subsidiary books (Theory), Cash Book (Simple and Columnar), Trial Balance.

Unit 3: Final Accounts: Trading and P&L Account, Balance Sheet of sole trading concerns with basic adjustments (Stock, Accrued, Prepaid, Depreciation).

Unit 4: Company & Sustainability: Kinds of company, Share Capital, Financial Statements per Companies Act 2013. Green Accounting and Sustainability Reporting.

3. Business Statistics and Logic (MG1CCRBBA102)
Credits: 4 | Core Course

Summary: Focuses on quantitative aptitude, arithmetic precision, and statistical tools for data-driven business decisions.

Course Outcomes:

Outline statistical concepts in business contexts.

Demonstrate data handling and logical reasoning skills.

Evaluate and interpret data using statistical models.

Course Content:

Unit 1: Central Tendency & Dispersion: Mean, Median, Mode, Weighted Mean. Range, Quartile Deviation, Mean Deviation, Standard Deviation, Skewness, and Kurtosis.

Unit 2: Correlation & Regression: Karl Pearson’s and Spearman’s Rank correlation. Regression lines (X on Y, Y on X) and equations.

Unit 3: Probability: Basic concepts, Addition/Multiplication rules. Distributions (Binomial, Poisson, Normal).

Unit 4: Logic: Number series, coding-decoding, direction sense, seating arrangements (linear/circular), blood relations, progressions.

4. Business Communication - I (MG1CCRBBA103)
Credits: 3 | Core Course

Summary: Instilling effective communication skills for the digital, multicultural corporate world.

Course Content:

Unit 1: Organizational Communication: Models, 7Cs of communication, formal/informal channels, barriers, and listening skills.

Unit 2: Written Communication: Layout of Business letters, emails, sales letters, complaint/follow-up letters, job applications, resumes, and resignation letters.

Unit 3: Interpersonal Communication: Team communication (online/virtual), Presentation skills (verbal/non-verbal), Infographics (Prezi, Zoho, etc.).

Unit 4: Digital Communication: Social media literacy, Video conferencing etiquette, Digital collaboration, and personal/organizational websites.

5. Indian Systems of Health and Wellness (MG1MDEBBA100)
Credits: 2 | Multidisciplinary Elective

Summary: Awareness of physical/mental well-being, lifestyle diseases, and Indian traditional health systems (AYUSH).

Course Content:

Unit 1: Health & Wellness: Definitions, components (Physical, Social, Mental, Spiritual), and determinants of health behavior.

Unit 2: Mind-Body Connection: Digital well-being and the importance of mental health.

Unit 3: Deficiency & Diseases: Malnutrition, sedentary lifestyle risks, and modern lifestyle health risks.

Unit 4: Indian Systems: Health beliefs of India, AYUSH systems, and indigenous perspectives on happiness.
